    The entire inspiration behind the creation of this planner is the one problem we all have deep down inside of us: procrastination. A couple months ago I watched this TED talk given by a man named Tim Urban, and the things he said were immensely relatable. The gist of the talk is that we all have a "rational decision maker" inside of us guiding our every move, but inside the mind of a procrastinator, there is also another actor: the "instant gratification monkey", who only sees short term benefit compared to the long-term goals a rational decision maker is able to set and work towards. Urban contends that in a procrastinator's world, this instant gratification monkey will always win, unless there is a situation involving an approaching deadline, in which the monkey is scared off, allowing the rational decision maker to take back the wheel. I thought to myself, in the case of homework, is there a way to visualize a deadline to curb procrastination tendancies? For the planner, the answer is quite simple: the deadline is the time the user needs to start his or her assignments in order to finish at a reasonable timeâ€”their bedtime. This can be 10:30, 11:00, or even midnight, but I hypothesize that in most scenarios getting a good night sleep, in theory, is appealing to any student. No one wants to be pulling all-nighters because of Netflix!
